THE ABD'S — Passing & Receiving
Angle, Body shape, Distance, and Scanning — four key factors for passing and receiving.
A
Angles
Support at a Diagonal
Never stand directly behind the ball carrier. Offer a diagonal so you can receive, turn, and play forward. Two support angles makes the team hard to press.
B
Body Shape
Be Ready Before You Receive
Open your body before the ball arrives. A half-turn lets you see the field and play in any direction. Decide before you receive — your body position tells teammates your next move.
D
Distance
Not Too Close, Not Too Far
Close enough to receive under pressure, far enough to stretch the defense. Too close removes a superior position. Read the pressure and adjust.
S
Scanning
Know What's Around You
Scan your shoulders before the ball arrives, not after. If you have not scanned you may be outnumbered without knowing it. Know the picture early.
COACHING TIP Remember your ABD'S — Angle, Body shape, Distance, Checking. All four work together. Get your angle right, open your body, hold the right distance, and scan early. Do all four and you become one of the easiest players to play with.
