TRANSITIONS — Moments That Win Games
The moment possession changes is when games are won and lost. React immediately — with purpose.
P
Positive Transition
We Win the Ball · Attack Immediately
If there's space, go. Attack with speed and commit defenders. If we can't go forward immediately, secure possession and build. Hold the ball with purpose to create the moment to go forward. On the turnover, stay balanced. Not everyone goes forward — some stretch, some support, some provide width, some stay behind. Always give the ball-winner clear options and maintain proper spacing.
N
Negative Transition
We Lose the Ball · React in 5 Seconds
The moment we lose the ball — react immediately. Counter-press with urgency and intent. Every player applies pressure within 5 seconds to win it back before the opponent can organize. If we can't win it back right away, recover our shape immediately. Get compact. Protect the middle and deny forward progress. Get goalside and communicate. Don't chase, don't over-defend. Stay balanced and patient, and force play where we want it.
COACHING TIP Win the ball back fast when lost — react immediately and counter-press as a unit. Find a pass to relieve pressure when won — look forward first, then build if needed.
