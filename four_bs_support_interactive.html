THE 4 B'S — Of Support
Behind, Beside, Between, Beyond — four positions of support that cover every situation in possession.
B1
Behind
The Safety Pass
Position yourself behind the ball carrier so if they can't play forward, they have a safe pass to start again. Goalkeepers and central defenders are well placed here. With experienced players, support diagonally behind — not straight — so the GK can play around quicker.
B2
Beside
Width in Possession
Support beside the ball carrier — inline, slightly behind, or slightly ahead depending on the pressure. Full backs often provide this option. The right decision depends on the type of pressure and how secure possession is.
B3
Between
Break the Lines
Midfielders position between the defensive lines — pinning the opposition back or flattening their press. This creates space to pass forward and break lines. The hardest position to defend against when timed right.
B4
Beyond
The Best Pass
The space beyond the last defender — the hardest pass but the best outcome. It puts the forward in front of goal. Even threatening beyond stretches the opposition and creates space for others to receive between the lines.
COACHING TIP Every time you're off the ball — ask yourself: am I Behind, Beside, Between, or Beyond? One of those four positions is always available. Find it before the ball arrives.
